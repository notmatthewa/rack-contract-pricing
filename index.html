Rack Price is a price that is not locked in by a contract. This means that the buyer will have to pay the market price at the time of purchase.

Offset Price Contract is a contract that allows the buyer to lock in an offset price for a specific period of time. This guarantees the buyer a lower price than the market price for the entire period of the contract.

Fixed Price Contract is a contract that allows the buyer to lock in a fixed price for a specific period of time. This guarantees the buyer a fixed price for the entire period of the contract, regardless of the market price being higher or lower than the fixed price.

Collar Price Contract is a contract that allows the buyer to lock in a minimum and maximum price for a specific period of time. This guarantees the buyer a price within the range of the minimum and maximum price for the entire period of the contract.
